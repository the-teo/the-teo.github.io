in this article, I'm going to talk about a simple method used to create a multiboot USB.

All you need to follow me is ventoy.

Download Ventoy

First, let's get the tool for it.

https://www.ventoy.net/en/index.html

Choose the right one for your system:

After the download is finished, open it and install.

Make the multiboot USB

Now, is the time to get your USB stick.

After you plug the USB in, start ventoy's app.

Now, we choose the USB stick you want to make multiboot.

I'll advise you to have secure boot support checked.

Option>Secure Boot Support

Now hit install and you're finished

Add operating systems to your USB

For you to be able to add OS on your USB stick, you only need to download them and then move them on your stick, just as you would do with any file.

This is how mine looks like:

Use Ventoy

Now that you have a multiboot USB, you can play around with different operating systems.

Now, restart your PC and get into your boot menu (this is different depending on your motherboard so you can search how online, most of the time is by repeatedly hitting f11)

Then choose your USB stick from the list.

Now you are in the ventoy welcome screen. This can be personalized, maybe I will explain how in another post.

Update ventoy on your USB stick

To update ventoy on your USB plug it in, open ventoy and hit UPDATE.

This WON'T DELETE your files.
